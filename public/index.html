Physical Discipline

Build strength, health, and self-control through consistent action. This pillar covers training, nutrition, sleep, and recovery—the fundamentals that make you resilient.

  • Train with intention (progressive overload, conditioning, mobility)
  • Eat for performance and clarity; track what matters
  • Prioritize sleep and simple recovery habits
Responsibility to Others

Discipline includes service. Measure how you support, protect, and elevate the people around you—family, friends, teammates, neighbors.

  • Keep promises; be reliable
  • Give time, attention, and practical help
  • Resolve conflicts quickly and fairly
Belief System & Morality

Anchor your life in a creed and live by it daily. Choose principle over comfort; cultivate humility, gratitude, and courage.

  • Write a short creed (3–5 lines) and review it weekly
  • Journal, pray, or meditate every day
  • Take the hard, right action when it counts
Skill & Mastery

Pick one valuable skill. Stick with it until you become exceptional. Track deliberate practice and ship visible work.

  • Choose 1 main pursuit; set a bi-weekly hour target
  • Ship one measurable output (demo, draft, PR, set, piece)
  • Seek feedback → iterate
Self-Reliance

Increase independence and capability. Learn to solve problems, manage time and money, and make sound decisions under pressure.

  • Own your schedule and finances
  • Build basic technical, trade, and survival skills
  • Document systems so you can teach others
Integrity & Consistency

Do what you said you would do—especially when it’s inconvenient. Small, repeatable actions compound into character.

  • Track promises and completions
  • Use simple checklists and habit chains
  • When you miss, reset fast and continue
