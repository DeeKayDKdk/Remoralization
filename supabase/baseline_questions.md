# Remoralization baseline questions

## Principle
Diagnosis before motivation.
Fix the weakest pillar first.

## Order

### 1. Physical Order
Q1. Which best matches the current state of your main living space?
Q2. How many unresolved physical friction points are currently in your daily environment?
Q3. If a sharp, disciplined person unexpectedly inspected your room, car, and daily setup today, how would it likely rate?

### 2. Time / Digital Order
Q4. How many days in the last 7 did you clearly know your plan before the day began?
Q5. Which best describes your calendar and planning system right now?
Q6. How many hours per day do you usually lose to unplanned phone/computer drift?

## Responsibility

### 3. Duties / Finance / Maintenance
Q7. How many known obligations are currently overdue, delayed, or being avoided?
Q8. Which best describes your financial and administrative control right now?
Q9. In the last 30 days, how often have you created avoidable problems through delay, sloppiness, or failure to handle something early?

### 4. Relationships / Communication
Q10. How many important messages or conversations are currently delayed because you have been avoiding them?
Q11. Which best describes your communication in important relationships?
Q12. If the people closest to you rated your dependability today, what would it most likely be?

## Mastery

### 5. Deliberate Practice
Q13. How many focused sessions have you completed in your main pursuit in the last 7 days?
Q14. Which best describes your current practice structure?
Q15. How many hours of deep, deliberate improvement work did you do in the last 7 days?

### 6. Proof of Progress / Standards
Q16. Do you currently have a measurable benchmark in your main pursuit?
Q17. In the last 30 days, do you have clear proof that you improved?
Q18. How often are you exposed to people, standards, or examples clearly above your level?

### 7. Clarity of Choice
Q19. Have you clearly chosen a main pursuit right now?
Q20. How well do you understand what your chosen path will actually demand over time?
Q21. Is your current path a considered commitment or mainly a mood, fantasy, or comparison-driven choice?

### 8. Meaning / Value
Q22. Would you still want this path if nobody saw you doing it?
Q23. Would becoming excellent at this make you more useful, capable, or valuable in real life?
Q24. Is this path worth the cost because of the value it can create?

## Physical Discipline

### 9. Body / Capacity
Q25. Which range best matches your current body-fat level?
Q26. Which range best matches your current one-mile ability?
Q27. How many of the last 7 days did you follow your intended standards for sleep, food, and training?

### 10. Self-Command
Q28. In the last 7 days, how often did you knowingly indulge impulses that weakened your standards?
Q29. Which best describes your current level of self-command?
Q30. If someone watched your daily habits for 14 days, what would they conclude?
